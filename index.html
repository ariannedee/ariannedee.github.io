Who am I?

My name is Arianne and I'm applying for the Makerlabs residency.

You can check out my maker experience or see my CV on LinkedIn.

I have been a crafter since I was a child. At summer camp, you could find me making bracelets and dream catchers with the councillors rather than playing with the other kids.

In university I studied Civil Engineering, and too late into the degree did I realize that they didn't actually get to build anything. I would have to become a construction worker for that. Not even the mini architectural models? No, you don't make those either.

Fast forward ten years, and I am working as a software developer. Even though I don't love computers, I enjoy the problem solving and the the ease of creating something from nothing. But I can't shake the feeling that there's something missing when you can't touch what you've built. It's all just ones and zeroes.

Why do I want to do a residency at MakerLabs?

Because I am at a cross-roads in my career. I have always loved making things and have been struggling for years to find the time to do it. Now I want to see if I can make it more than just a hobby.

Having spent the last four years as a software developer, working for other people, I am ready for a change.

  • I want to not sit at a desk all day
  • I want to work directly with the people that I am building things for
  • I want to work with more women

Before hearing about this residency, I had already committed to leaving my job and trying to make a go of it on my own, with the assortment of skills I have acquired so far in life. Programming, teaching, sewing, and woodworking were the primary candidates.

While I know that I can code and teach for money, this residency would allow me to see if I can turn what I love as a hobby into a career (or at least pay for itself + ramen).

Last Thursday was my last day of work and I'll be dedicating the next 6 months to self-employment. I would love the Makerlabs community, to be a part of it.

What I would like to get out of the residency:

  • Dedicated time and space to pursue a maker career
  • A collaborative environment with other women
  • Mentorship, words of encouragement, and feedback

What I have to offer:

  • Good at brainstorming and coming up with creative solutions
  • Positive attitude, and am not afraid to try things or make mistakes
  • Can help others with coding (Python or HTML/CSS/Javascript), laser cutting, sewing, and woodworking

What I want to learn:

  • Welding & metalworking
  • Using the CNC router
  • Lots more woodworking skills and practise, including the lathe
  • Time and project management skills

Other tools I would be using:

  • Woodshop tools: jointer/planer, table saw, mitre saw
  • Laser cutter
  • Serger, if there is one
